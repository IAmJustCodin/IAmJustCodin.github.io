Surgical Strike (Uri)

On September 18, 2016, four heavily armed terrorists attacked an Indian Army camp in Uri, Jammu & Kashmir, killing 19 soldiers.The attackers were linked to Pakistan-based terrorist group Jaish-e-Mohammed. This deadly assault provoked national outrage and was seen as a major security failure.

In retaliation, the Indian Army conducted a surgical strike on the night of September 28–29, 2016.Elite Para Special Forces crossed the Line of Control (LoC) into Pakistan-occupied Kashmir and targeted 7 terrorist launch pads — temporary camps where terrorists were preparing to infiltrate into India.

The operation was planned with high precision, using intelligence, surveillance drones, and satellite images.The soldiers returned safely without any casualties, and the mission was considered a tactical and strategic success by Indian authorities.

This was the first time India publicly acknowledged a cross-border strike of this nature.It marked a shift in India’s military doctrine — from passive defense to proactive and offensive counter-terrorism.It also significantly raised domestic morale, received widespread public support, and sent a strong message to Pakistan and the international community.
